Kenyan Chapati & Beans

Coastal-inspired comfort food

This hearty combination showcases Kenya's coastal influences with soft, flaky chapatis served with creamy coconut beans. A staple in Mombasa households with Swahili roots.

Prep: 30 mins (plus soaking) Cook: 1.5 hours Serves: 6

Ingredients

For the Beans:

  • 2 cups dried red kidney beans, soaked overnight
  • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp ginger, grated
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tbsp pilau masala
  • 2 cups vegetable stock
  • Salt to taste

For the Chapati:

  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 1/2 cups warm water
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • Extra flour for dusting

Instructions

  1. Cook the Beans:

    Drain soaked beans and boil in fresh water for 45 minutes until tender. Drain and set aside.

  2. In a pot, sauté onions until translucent. Add garlic, ginger, and spices, cooking for 1 minute until fragrant.

  3. Add tomatoes and cook until softened. Stir in cooked beans, coconut milk, and stock. Simmer for 30 minutes until creamy.

  4. Make the Chapati:

    Mix flour, salt, and sugar. Gradually add water to form a soft dough. Knead for 10 minutes until elastic.

  5. Divide into 12 balls. Roll each into a circle, brush with oil, fold into quarters, then roll out again to 1/4" thickness.

  6. Cook on a hot skillet for 1 minute per side, brushing with oil until golden brown spots appear.

  7. Serve:

    Plate chapati with a generous serving of beans. Coastal style includes a side of kachumbari (tomato-onion salad) and chili sauce.

Treezer's Tips

Swahili Secret

For authentic Mombasa flavor, add 1 tbsp of tamarind paste to the beans during cooking.

Chapati Technique

The key to flaky layers is the folding technique and resting the dough for 30 minutes before rolling.

Time-Saver

Use canned beans and coconut cream for a quicker version - simmer for just 15 minutes.

Nutritional Information

Per serving: 580 calories | 18g protein | 85g carbs | 20g fat | High in fiber, iron, and healthy fats
